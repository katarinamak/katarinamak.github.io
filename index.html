About Me

Hi! I’m Katarina and I recently graduated from the University of Waterloo with a degree in Computer Science. Over the past few years, I’ve worked at a wide range of companies and industries, and I’ve found myself most drawn to the challenge of building systems that reliably store and manage ever-growing volumes of data. As the world produces more data than ever before, one of the most important questions in tech is how we store, move, and make use of that data in ways that are reliable, efficient, and meaningful. I want to contribute to building and designing systems that can keep up with the rapid pace of change, while shaping how we use technology to solve real-world problems.


I’m especially drawn to backend development because it gives me a chance to think deeply and build things that really work. I enjoy approaching problems analytically, weighing different options, and designing solutions that are both practical and robust. My goal is to contribute to work that has real impact and to collaborate with people who care about both the technology and the purpose behind it.


Outside of work, I’m always learning and exploring. I love running, hiking, reading, baking, playing the piano, and traveling. I'm a big believer in balance, and I’ve found that contrasting my very technical work with creative hobbies helps keep me curious and inspired.


If any of this resonates with you, I’d love to connect and hear your thoughts. Please feel free to reach out!

Resume

Backend Software Development Engineer Intern, Amazon AWS DynamoDB

Jan 2024 – Aug 2024

What happens when the same data is stored in multiple parts of a system? Do we need multiple leaders to ensure consistency? How can we leverage existing tools to improve availability, efficiency, and durability? Can we adapt components of existing consensus algorithms to better suit DynamoDB’s unique architecture and workload?


These were some of the challenging questions I explored during my internship at AWS DynamoDB. My project centered on designing and implementing a new leaderless replication protocol for global secondary indexes. This experience deepened my understanding of the complex trade-offs involved in building high-throughput, distributed systems at scale. The new architecture I developed demonstrated promising results—improving write throughput and reducing index propagation latency by 30%, ultimately leading to stronger data consistency guarantees.

Amazon’s strong writing culture and rigorous design processes gave me the opportunity to drive technical discussions, collaborate with senior and principal engineers, and present trade-off analyses that shaped the final design of the system.

Fullstack Software Engineer Intern, Microsoft

At Microsoft, I worked on the Photos App, which is used by over half a billion users worldwide. My primary project involved designing and implementing a telemetry API from the ground up to capture user behavior, preferences, and performance metrics. Telemetry is essential for understanding product usage patterns and optimizing user experience, so it was critical to make thoughtful architectural decisions early on to ensure the API was scalable, reliable, and extensible as the app evolved. By building a reliable pipeline to collect high-quality telemetry data, I helped provide the foundation for data science teams to uncover meaningful insights and drive impactful product improvements. In my second project, I transitioned to the frontend, where I enhanced the Photos App's user interface by developing new features that allow users to select, share, and delete multiple photos and videos simultaneously. This significantly streamlined bulk media management and improved overall usability. Together, these experiences gave me a full-stack perspective - from backend systems design to user-facing UI development—and strengthened my skills in building impactful, user-centric software.

Backend Software Developer Intern, Auvik Networks

Jan 2023 – Apr 2023

On the Platform Data Lifecycle team at Auvik Networks, I focused on the backup, migration, and restoration of critical platform data. The system pulled data from multiple sources and storage formats, including relational databases, AWS S3, graph databases, and streams, which pushed me to think carefully about how data moves, transforms, and gets preserved across complex systems, as well as how different storage models support diverse system needs. To track the flow and transformation of data across services, I designed and implemented a data dependency graph that modeled how data moved through the system. This enabled teams to apply familiar graph traversal algorithms to assess the current state of the data and its progression through processing stages. In contributing to Auvik’s microservices-based architecture, I gained practical insight into service-oriented design and the tradeoffs compared to traditional monolithic systems. I also built experience with modern deployment and orchestration tools such as Docker and Kubernetes, which broadened my understanding of scalable infrastructure and DevOps workflows.

Backend Software Developer Intern, Royal Bank of Canada

Jan 2022 – Apr 2022

At RBC, I joined a team building a platform to automate conditional mortgage approvals. Since we were building the system from the ground up, it was important to consider the long-term impact of every design decision we were making. I contributed to scoping, developing, and testing end-to-end features, and played an active role in early discussions around feature requirements and relational database design with both engineers and business analysts. Being part of a product at this stage taught me to think beyond just immediate implementation and instead consider how systems evolve, how requirements shift, and how to design for growth and change. To help ensure our APIs were reliable as the system scaled, I implemented Pact contract tests across 60% of our services, catching integration issues before they hit production and improving our test coverage. It was a hands-on experience that taught me to think beyond just making things work and focus on building systems that are resilient, maintainable, and built to last.

Quality Assurance Intern, The Toronto Star

May 2021 – Aug 2021

Having been involved with my high school newspaper, I’ve always cared deeply about journalism and the role it plays in our society.
